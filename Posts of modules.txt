Subject: MScFE 632 | Module 2: Unsupervised Learning & Yield Curve PCA

Technical Takeaways from Module 2:

Clustering Dynamics: Mastering K-means and using the Elbow Plot to determine the optimal number of clusters—a vital tool for regime detection in volatile markets.

Hierarchical Structures: Using Dendrograms to visualize complex relationships between assets, providing a more granular view than traditional correlation matrices.

Dimensionality Reduction: Applying PCA to condense high-dimensional data. Understanding Loadings and Principal Components is essential for filtering market noise.

Yield Curve Modeling: A standout application was using PCA for Interest Rate Modeling. Analyzing the covariance of yield curves to calculate Value at Risk (VaR) demonstrates the direct impact of unsupervised learning on risk management.

MScFE 632 | Module 3: Classification Problems with Bayes Methods and Classification Trees
Technical Takeaways from Module 3:

Bayesian Statistics: Shifting from static point estimates to a dynamic framework of Priors, Likelihood, and Posteriors. This allows for the integration of expert financial knowledge into the model fitting process.
Naive Bayes in Practice: Utilizing Gaussian Distributions for model fitting. In high-dimensional financial markets, the assumption of independence offers a powerful baseline for classification.
Decision Trees: Moving from linear logic to non-linear partitioning. Mastering Gini Impurity to create Homogeneous Nodes has been key to understanding how trees segment market risk.
Ensemble & Performance: Exploring Boosting and evaluating robustness via the ROC Curve and Area Under the Curve (AUC). These metrics are vital for ensuring our models perform under real-world testing conditions.
